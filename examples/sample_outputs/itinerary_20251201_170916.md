### Day 1: Arrival in Tokyo & Shinjuku Immersion

**Geographic Zone:** Shinjuku

**Morning (10:00 AM - 12:00 PM): Arrival & Check-in**
*   **10:00 AM:** Arrive at Narita (NRT) or Haneda (HND) Airport.
*   **10:30 AM - 11:30 AM:** Clear immigration, collect luggage, and purchase/load your Suica card at the JR East Travel Service Center.
*   **11:30 AM - 1:00 PM:** Take the Narita Express (approx. ¬•3,000, 1.5 hrs) or Limousine Bus (approx. ¬•3,100, 1.5-2 hrs) to Shinjuku Station, or Keikyu Line/Limousine Bus from Haneda (approx. ¬•1,000-¬•1,500, 1 hr).
*   **1:00 PM:** Check into your hotel in the Shinjuku area for easy access to transport and attractions.

**Lunch Options (1:00 PM - 2:00 PM):**
1.  **T's Tantan (Tokyo Station, inside the station)** (¬•1,800) - Famous for its delicious, entirely vegetarian and vegan ramen. They have several broth bases, none spicy. *Dietary: Fully Vegetarian/Vegan, Non-Spicy.*
2.  **Ichiran Ramen (Shinjuku)** (¬•1,500) - While known for pork broth, they offer a special vegetarian broth option and allow customization of spice levels (choose 0 for no spice). You can also customize toppings. *Dietary: Vegetarian Broth Available, Non-Spicy Options.*
3.  **Ootoya (Shinjuku)** (¬•1,600) - A reliable chain restaurant offering set meals (teishoku). Many options can be made vegetarian (e.g., vegetable tempura set, tofu steak set). Inform staff clearly about "no spicy" and "vegetarian." *Dietary: Vegetarian Options Available, Can Be Prepared Non-Spicy.*

**Afternoon (2:00 PM - 6:00 PM): Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden & Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building**
*   **2:00 PM - 4:00 PM:** **Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden** (¬•500 entrance). Explore its diverse garden styles: English Landscape, French Formal, and Japanese Traditional. It's a beautiful spot, especially with early cherry blossoms. (Approx. 15-min walk from Shinjuku Station South Exit).
*   **4:15 PM - 5:30 PM:** **Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building Observation Decks** (FREE). Head to the North or South Tower for panoramic views of Tokyo. It‚Äôs a great way to orient yourself. (Approx. 10-min walk from Shinjuku Station West Exit).

**Dinner Options (6:30 PM - 8:00 PM):**
1.  **Afuri Ramen (Shinjuku)** (¬•1,400) - Known for its refreshing, lighter Yuzu Shio ramen. They offer a dedicated vegetarian ramen with a clear broth base, and it's inherently not spicy. *Dietary: Dedicated Vegetarian Ramen, Non-Spicy.*
2.  **Udon Shin (Shinjuku)** (¬•1,800) - Freshly made udon noodles. Their hot udon broth is a dashi-based (kelp/mushroom for vegetarian) and can be ordered without spicy toppings. Excellent vegetarian options like vegetable tempura udon. *Dietary: Vegetarian Options Available, Non-Spicy.*
3.  **AIN SOPH Journey (Shinjuku)** (¬•2,500) - A popular 100% vegan restaurant offering a variety of Western-style dishes like pancakes, burgers, and curries. All dishes are naturally vegetarian and non-spicy. *Dietary: Fully Vegetarian/Vegan, Non-Spicy.*

**Evening (8:00 PM onwards): Shinjuku Nightlife & Golden Gai**
*   **8:00 PM - 9:30 PM:** Explore the vibrant neon-lit streets of Shinjuku.
*   **9:30 PM onwards:** Wander through **Omoide Yokocho** (Memory Lane) for a nostalgic, atmospheric experience (small yakitori stalls, but some offer grilled vegetables). Then, experience the unique tiny bars of **Golden Gai**. Many bars have cover charges, and some are more welcoming to tourists than others. Stick to bars that look inviting and check for signs.

**Geographic Flow:** Arrival ‚Üí Hotel ‚Üí Garden ‚Üí City Hall ‚Üí Dinner ‚Üí Evening Exploration. All within Shinjuku.
**Transport:** Airport transfer + Walking. Suica usage minimal today.
**Budget (Estimate):** ¬•15,000 (Transport) + ¬•7,000 (Food) + ¬•500 (Garden) = ¬•22,500
**Booking Required:** None.
**Weather Backup:** If rain, spend more time at Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building or explore department store food halls (Depachika) for variety.
**Pro Tips:**
*   Get your Suica card ready at the airport.
*   Shinjuku Station is massive; familiarize yourself with the South, West, and East exits.
*   Golden Gai can be intimidating; approach with an open mind and respect the intimate atmosphere.

### Day 2: Tradition & Trends in Asakusa & Ueno

**Geographic Zone:** Asakusa, Ueno

**Morning (8:30 AM - 12:30 PM): Asakusa's Historic Charm**
*   **8:30 AM - 9:00 AM:** Travel via Tokyo Metro Ginza Line to Asakusa Station.
*   **9:00 AM - 11:00 AM:** **Senso-ji Temple.** Enter through the Kaminarimon Gate, walk down Nakamise-dori market (great for souvenirs, try vegetarian senbei crackers), and explore the temple grounds. Arrive early to avoid crowds.
*   **11:00 AM - 12:00 PM:** Stroll along the Sumida River for views of the Tokyo Skytree and the unique Asahi Beer Hall building.
*   **12:00 PM - 12:30 PM:** Walk towards the nearby **Kappabashi Kitchen Town** (optional, for interesting kitchenware shops).

**Lunch Options (12:30 PM - 1:30 PM):**
1.  **Daikokuya Tempura (Asakusa)** (¬•2,000) - Famous for tendon (tempura on rice). Request a vegetarian tendon bowl with assorted vegetables (yasai tendon). Ensure they confirm it's not cooked in the same oil as meat/fish if strict. *Dietary: Vegetarian Tempura Option Available, Non-Spicy.*
2.  **Asakusa Okonomiyaki Sometaro (Asakusa)** (¬•1,800) - A traditional okonomiyaki restaurant where you cook your own. Order the vegetable-only okonomiyaki (yasai) and ask for no dashi in the batter if needed (confirm ingredients). *Dietary: Vegetarian Okonomiyaki, Can Be Prepared Non-Spicy.*
3.  **Saido (Jiyugaoka, a bit further)** (¬•3,000) - If you're willing to travel slightly for a top-tier vegetarian experience, Saido is renowned for its exquisite vegan tasting menus. (Requires JR Yamanote line + Tokyu Toyoko line - ~45 mins travel). *Dietary: Fully Vegan, Non-Spicy.*

**Afternoon (1:30 PM - 5:30 PM): Ueno Park's Cultural Hub**
*   **1:30 PM - 2:00 PM:** Travel via Tokyo Metro Ginza Line from Asakusa to Ueno Station (approx. 5 mins).
*   **2:00 PM - 4:00 PM:** **Ueno Park.** Explore its vast grounds. Depending on your interest:
    *   **Tokyo National Museum** (¬•1,000): Japan's largest museum, housing a vast collection of Japanese art and artifacts.
    *   **Tokyo Metropolitan Art Museum** (Free general admission, special exhibits vary): Features rotating exhibitions.
    *   Or simply enjoy the park atmosphere and potential cherry blossoms.
*   **4:00 PM - 5:30 PM:** Wander through **Ameya Yokocho (Ameyoko Market)**, a bustling street market under the train tracks, offering everything from fresh produce to clothing.

**Dinner Options (6:00 PM - 7:30 PM):**
1.  **Ueno Yabusoba (Ueno)** (¬•1,700) - Traditional soba noodle restaurant. They offer vegetarian soba dishes like tempura soba (ensure vegetable tempura) or zaru soba (cold noodles with dipping sauce). *Dietary: Vegetarian Soba Options, Non-Spicy.*
2.  **Gyoza Lou (Harajuku, a short train ride away)** (¬•1,500) - Known for delicious pan-fried dumplings. They offer a vegetable gyoza and have plain boiled options too. Their dipping sauce is vinegar-based, not spicy. *Dietary: Vegetable Gyoza, Non-Spicy.*
3.  **Shinjuku's Ain Soph Journey (If returning to Shinjuku)** (¬•2,500) - Reliable vegan option. *Dietary: Fully Vegetarian/Vegan, Non-Spicy.*

**Evening (7:30 PM onwards): Yanaka Ginza or Akihabara Exploration**
*   **Option A (Traditional Charm):** Take the JR Yamanote Line to Nippori Station and explore **Yanaka Ginza**, a charming, old-fashioned shopping street that offers a glimpse into pre-war Tokyo. Enjoy local snacks and the laid-back atmosphere.
*   **Option B (Electric Town):** Take the JR Yamanote Line to Akihabara. Experience the dazzling lights, electronics stores, anime shops, and arcades. It's a sensory overload in the best way.

**Geographic Flow:** Hotel ‚Üí Asakusa ‚Üí Ueno ‚Üí Market ‚Üí Dinner ‚Üí Yanaka/Akihabara.
**Transport:** Tokyo Metro (Ginza Line), JR Yamanote Line. Suica essential.
**Budget (Estimate):** ¬•1,500 (Transport) + ¬•7,000 (Food) + ¬•1,000 (Museum) = ¬•9,500
**Booking Required:** None.
**Weather Backup:** Spend more time in museums in Ueno Park.
**Pro Tips:**
*   Nakamise-dori can be crowded; focus on the temple itself early on.
*   Ameyoko Market is great for cheap snacks and people-watching.
*   Yanaka Ginza offers a peaceful contrast to the bustle of Ueno and Akihabara.

### Day 3: Imperial Grandeur & Pop Culture Hubs

**Geographic Zone:** Chiyoda (Imperial Palace), Harajuku, Shibuya

**Morning (9:00 AM - 12:30 PM): Imperial Palace East Garden & Surroundings**
*   **9:00 AM - 9:30 AM:** Travel via Tokyo Metro Chiyoda Line to Otemachi Station.
*   **9:30 AM - 11:30 AM:** **Imperial Palace East Garden (Kokyo Higashi Gyoen)** (FREE). Explore the former site of Edo Castle, including the massive stone walls, moats, and guardhouses. (Note: The inner palace grounds are generally not accessible except by guided tour booked far in advance).
*   **11:30 AM - 12:30 PM:** Walk around the **Marunouchi district** near Tokyo Station, known for its sophisticated architecture and upscale shops.

**Lunch Options (12:30 PM - 1:30 PM):**
1.  **Tokyo Station Ramen Street** (¬•1,500) - Located within Tokyo Station, offers various ramen styles. Look for vegetarian options (e.g., T's Tantan mentioned earlier, or others that specify vegetable broth). *Dietary: Varies by shop, check menus for Vegetarian/Non-Spicy.*
2.  **Soranoiro NIPPON (Tokyo Station)** (¬•1,600) - Known for its colorful and healthy vegetable ramen. They have creative vegan and vegetarian options. *Dietary: Vegetarian/Vegan, Non-Spicy.*
3.  **Affordable Cafes in Marunouchi Building/Shin-Marunouchi Building** (¬•1,800) - Many cafes offer salads, sandwiches, and pasta that can be vegetarian and non-spicy. *Dietary: Varies, check menus.*

**Afternoon (1:30 PM - 5:30 PM): Harajuku's Youth Culture & Meiji Shrine**
*   **1:30 PM - 2:00 PM:** Travel via JR Yamanote Line from Tokyo Station to Harajuku Station.
*   **2:00 PM - 3:30 PM:** **Meiji Jingu Shrine.** Escape the city buzz in this serene forest sanctuary dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken. (Located right next to Harajuku Station).
*   **3:30 PM - 5:30 PM:** Immerse yourself in **Takeshita Street**, the epicenter of Tokyo's vibrant youth fashion and quirky street food. Explore side streets for vintage shops and unique boutiques.

**Dinner Options (6:00 PM - 7:30 PM):**
1.  **Harajuku Gyozaro (Harajuku)** (¬•1,200) - Famous for its simple, delicious gyoza (dumplings). Offers vegetable gyoza and plain boiled options. Non-spicy dipping sauce. Very popular and budget-friendly. *Dietary: Vegetable Gyoza, Non-Spicy.*
2.  **Saido (Jiyugaoka - requires travel)** (¬•3,000) - If you missed it earlier and want a refined vegan experience. *Dietary: Fully Vegan, Non-Spicy.*
3.  **Veganic To Go (Shibuya)** (¬•1,800) - Located near Shibuya, offers healthy vegan bowls, salads, and smoothies. *Dietary: Fully Vegetarian/Vegan, Non-Spicy.*

**Evening (7:30 PM onwards): Shibuya Scramble & Views**
*   **7:30 PM - 9:00 PM:** Walk or take the JR Yamanote Line one stop from Harajuku to Shibuya Station. Experience the iconic **Shibuya Crossing**. Watch the organized chaos from the Starbucks overlooking the intersection (can be crowded) or the Mag's Park rooftop at Magnet by Shibuya 109.
*   **9:00 PM onwards:** Explore Shibuya's nightlife, including the Hachiko statue area, Center Gai street, and numerous izakayas (Japanese pubs) and bars. Many offer non-spicy vegetarian small plates.

**Geographic Flow:** Hotel ‚Üí Imperial Palace area ‚Üí Harajuku (Shrine then Street) ‚Üí Shibuya.
**Transport:** Tokyo Metro (Chiyoda Line), JR Yamanote Line. Suica essential.
**Budget (Estimate):** ¬•1,500 (Transport) + ¬•7,000 (Food) + ¬•0 (Gardens/Shrine/Crossing) = ¬•8,500
**Booking Required:** None.
**Weather Backup:** If heavy rain, focus on indoor shopping in Marunouchi, Shibuya 109, or department stores.
**Pro Tips:**
*   Wear comfortable shoes as you'll be doing a lot of walking.
*   Takeshita Street is best explored during daylight hours for the full visual impact.
*   Shibuya Crossing is mesmerizing at night.

### Day 4: Cherry Blossom Viewing & Artistic Flair

**Geographic Zone:** Ueno (revisit), Yanaka, Nezu

**Morning (9:00 AM - 12:30 PM): Cherry Blossom Spectacle at Ueno Park**
*   **9:00 AM - 9:30 AM:** Travel via JR Yamanote Line or Tokyo Metro to Ueno Station.
*   **9:30 AM - 12:30 PM:** **Ueno Park Cherry Blossom Viewing (Hanami).** April 1st is prime time! Stroll under the canopy of cherry blossoms along the main pathway. Visit Shinobazu Pond. If you're interested, you could pop into the Tokyo National Museum again briefly. Consider picking up some vegetarian snacks from a nearby convenience store or bakery for a picnic.

**Lunch Options (12:30 PM - 1:30 PM):**
1.  **Izuei Hana no Tei (Ueno Park)** (¬•2,500) - Offers various set meals, including options with tempura and grilled fish. Request a vegetarian option like vegetable tempura or tofu set. Confirm ingredients. *Dietary: Vegetarian Options, Check for Non-Spicy.*
2.  **Various vendors in Ameyoko Market** (¬•1,500) - Many stalls offer fruits, crepes, sweet potato snacks, and sometimes vegetable-based street food. Choose wisely. *Dietary: Varies, focus on fruits, sweets.*
3.  **Ueno Station Area Cafes** (¬•1,800) - Similar to Marunouchi, many cafes offer safe vegetarian, non-spicy options like pasta or sandwiches. *Dietary: Varies, check menus.*

**Afternoon (1:30 PM - 5:30 PM): Yanaka's Old Tokyo Charm & Nezu Shrine**
*   **1:30 PM - 2:00 PM:** Walk (approx. 20 mins) or take a short bus ride from Ueno to Yanaka Station/Yanaka Ginza area.
*   **2:00 PM - 3:30 PM:** Explore **Yanaka Ginza** shopping street again, perhaps trying some local sweets or browsing shops at a relaxed pace. Visit the nearby **Yanaka Cemetery**, which is surprisingly beautiful, especially during cherry blossom season.
*   **3:30 PM - 5:00 PM:** Walk (approx. 15 mins) to **Nezu Shrine**. This stunning shrine is famous for its tunnel of red torii gates (similar to Kyoto's Fushimi Inari) and beautiful azaleas (though they bloom slightly later, the shrine itself is worth visiting).
*   **5:00 PM - 5:30 PM:** Enjoy a peaceful moment at Nezu Shrine.

**Dinner Options (6:00 PM - 7:30 PM):**
1.  **Afuri Ramen (Ebisu or Shinjuku)** (¬•1,400) - If you enjoyed it, consider visiting another branch. Ebisu offers a slightly more upscale vibe. *Dietary: Dedicated Vegetarian Ramen, Non-Spicy.*
2.  **Komachi Cafe (Shinjuku Lumine Est)** (¬•2,000) - Offers Japanese-style vegetarian and vegan dishes, often with beautiful presentation. *Dietary: Fully Vegetarian/Vegan, Non-Spicy.*
3.  **Ippudo Ramen (Various locations, e.g., Shinjuku, Shibuya)** (¬•1,500) - While primarily known for tonkotsu, they often have a vegetarian-friendly "yasai" (vegetable) ramen option. Confirm broth and spice level. *Dietary: Vegetarian Option Available, Confirm Non-Spicy.*

**Evening (7:30 PM onwards): Shimo-Kitazawa Exploration**
*   **7:30 PM - 8:00 PM:** Travel via JR Yamanote Line to Shinjuku, then transfer to the Odakyu Line or Keio Inokashira Line to **Shimo-Kitazawa** station (approx. 30-40 mins total travel).
*   **8:00 PM onwards:** Explore Shimo-Kitazawa, a trendy neighborhood known for its vintage clothing stores, independent boutiques, cozy cafes, and lively bars and live music venues. It has a more bohemian and relaxed vibe compared to Shibuya or Shinjuku. Find a small, quirky bar for a drink.

**Geographic Flow:** Hotel ‚Üí Ueno Park ‚Üí Yanaka ‚Üí Nezu Shrine ‚Üí Shimo-Kitazawa.
**Transport:** JR Yamanote Line, Tokyo Metro, Odakyu/Keio Lines. Suica essential.
**Budget (Estimate):** ¬•2,000 (Transport) + ¬•7,000 (Food) + ¬•0 (Hanami/Shrines/Gardens) = ¬•9,000
**Booking Required:** None.
**Weather Backup:** Explore museums in Ueno, or spend more time browsing shops in Yanaka Ginza or Shimo-Kitazawa.
**Pro Tips:**
*   Ueno Park is massive; focus on the main cherry blossom avenue.
*   Yanaka Ginza feels like stepping back in time.
*   Shimo-Kitazawa is great for unique finds and a relaxed evening atmosphere.

### Day 5: Transition Day - Art, Views & Move to Odaiba

Borderless is closing and reopening in Azabudai Hills in early 2024 (check exact location/dates), Planets is in Toyosu (short train ride from Odaiba). *Note: Check the exact location and opening status for teamLab Borderless.*

### Day 6: Ghibli Magic & Trendy Neighborhoods

**Morning (9:00 AM - 12:30 PM): Studio Ghibli Museum**
*   **9:00 AM - 10:00 AM:** Travel from Odaiba to Mitaka. Take the Yurikamome Line back to Shimbashi, then JR Yamanote Line to Kichijoji Station, followed by a short walk or community bus (Ghibli Bus) to the museum. *   **10:00 AM - 12:30 PM:** **Ghibli Museum, Mitaka** (¬•1,000 - *Tickets are EXTREMELY hard to get and must be booked months in advance online*). Immerse yourself in the whimsical world of Hayao Miyazaki's animated films. Photography is not allowed inside.

**Lunch Options (12:30 PM - 1:30 PM):**
1.  **Straw Hat Cafe (Ghibli Museum)** (¬•2,000) - Located inside the museum, offers simple, charming dishes. Their menu is generally vegetarian-friendly (e.g., simple pasta, curry rice). *Dietary: Vegetarian Options, Non-Spicy.*
2.  **Cafes near Kichijoji Station** (¬•1,800) - Kichijoji has a plethora of lovely cafes and restaurants. Look for pasta, sandwiches, or Japanese set meals. *Dietary: Varies, check menus.*
3.  **Harmonica Yokocho (Kichijoji)** (¬•1,500) - A maze of tiny eateries offering various Japanese foods. Might find vegetable stir-fries or simple noodle dishes. *Dietary: Varies, requires searching.*

**Afternoon (1:30 PM - 5:30 PM): Kichijoji Exploration & Inokashira Park**
*   **1:30 PM - 3:30 PM:** Explore **Kichijoji**. It's consistently voted one of Tokyo's most desirable neighborhoods, with trendy shops, department stores (like Kirarina), and lively streets.
*   **3:30 PM - 5:00 PM:** Relax in **Inokashira Park**, adjacent to the Ghibli Museum. Enjoy the pond with swan boats, the small zoo, and the peaceful atmosphere. Depending on the exact bloom, it might still have some cherry blossoms.

**Dinner Options (6:00 PM - 7:30 PM):**
1.  **AIN SOPH Ripple (Shinjuku)** (¬•2,500) - Another branch of the vegan restaurant chain, convenient if heading back towards central Tokyo. *Dietary: Fully Vegetarian/Vegan, Non-Spicy.*
2.  **Shinjuku Omoide Yokocho stalls** (¬•2,000) - While known for yakitori, some stalls offer grilled vegetables (yasai itame, yaki tamago) or simple noodle dishes. Be very clear about vegetarian needs. *Dietary: Limited Vegetarian Options, Requires careful selection.*
3.  **Trendy Izakaya in Shimo-Kitazawa** (¬•2,500) - Revisit Shimo-Kitazawa or explore its restaurants for modern Japanese tapas-style dining. Many izakayas offer vegetable dishes like agedashi tofu, vegetable tempura, edamame, and salads. *Dietary: Good Vegetarian Options, ensure non-spicy.*

**Evening (7:30 PM onwards): Shimo-Kitazawa's Bohemian Nightlife**
*   **7:30 PM - 8:00 PM:** Travel from Kichijoji to Shimo-Kitazawa via the Keio Inokashira Line (direct connection).
*   **8:00 PM onwards:** Dive deeper into **Shimo-Kitazawa**. Enjoy its laid-back bars, live music venues, and independent theaters. It‚Äôs a great place for a relaxed evening.

**Geographic Flow:** Hotel ‚Üí Mitaka (Ghibli) ‚Üí Kichijoji ‚Üí Shimo-Kitazawa.
